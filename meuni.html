ME UNI.

A meditation app helping people keep meditating

OVERVIEW

Me Uni. is a mobile app aiming to help people build a habit of meditation. It features a fun and engaging game to motivate users to keep the practice, a robust social platform allowing users to connect to the meditation community, and a heart beat tracker to watch their body change. Through Me Uni., users can form a lasting habit of meditation, enjoy doing it alone or join the thriving community of meditators around the globe.

    BACKGORUND

    Meditation has long been under scientific investigation and proven beneficial to mental health. It is not just effective; even few minutes of meditation can improve resilience, it is also easy to learn. However, for people considering attending a meditation class, time and location are always two big challenges.

    Many meditation apps have brought their solutions to address those challenges. While after surveying the main players in the market, we found there are still some shortcomings within the current solutions. Therefore, we decided to propose our solution based on our research.

    COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

    We selected three direct competitors with most users and three indirect competitors as the targets of our competitive analysis. We listed the features they contain and compared the way they organize information. We also reviewed usersâ€™ comments and ratings. After those close examination, we were able to generalized some takeaways about what are provided and what are missed in the existing solutions.

    TAKEAWAYS

    After finishing the comparision, we summarized the results and distilled three takeaways.

    Most competitors provide a variety of content, such as audio, music, broadcast, even video. Some classify their content by different interests (e.g. anxiety, relationship). Some classify their content according to different groups of audience (e.g. begineer, kid). However, we found most of the content are quite similar - they are all about teaching the same techniques.

    Most competitors provide self tracking information such as the stats of continued time. To motivate users to keep practice, some of them use leaderboards, while some use badges. All competitors allow setting personal reminder (and usually will recommend to do so).

    Very few competitors include social community feature. Most of them have very limited channel to reach out other users or mentors. In this catalog, Insight Timer makes it standing out from the others. The social community on Insight Timer is strong and users become closer through experience sharing and interpersonal communication.

    PROBLEMS

    Although we've seen many features provided by the competitors, there are still some gaps we think should be filled. When talking about the goal of forming a lasting habit, the most challenging thing for a meditation app is how to keep users using it. We found several problems might occur when the current solutions try to achieve this goal.

    01

    HARD TO PERSIST

    Even though many gamified features included, beginners still can't persist to form a reqular habit

    02

    NOT MEET EXPECTAION

    The content with specific interest fails to discriminate it from the others. The interest may not be perceived

    03

    LACK OF SUPPORT

    There is no place for users to seek advices when they find something challenging with the practice

    04

    LACK OF MEASURE

    There is no physiological measurement to record the change of body for users to obtain senses of the benefits.

    BEHAVIROAL FACTORS

    In addition to competitive analysis, we also reviewed Social Network Theory and Intergrated Behavioral Model that are deprived from the discipline of public health. Such behavior theories or models have attempted to explain how people form their health behaviors.

    The theory and model gave us directions about how to trigger a behavior and how to keep the behavior. We found that motivation is the key factor for people to consider to change their health behaviors. While to from a long term health behavior, more factors come to play a role in the process, including self-efficacy, social support. Our solutions would focus on enhancing these three behavioral factors.

    01

    MOTIVATION

    Motivation is the trigger for the individual to consider bahavior change. It can be reinforced by creating feedback or reward, and foreseeing the positive outcome

    02

    SELF-EFFICACY

    Self-efficacy is the belief that the individual can perform a behavior. It can be enhanced by providing information, skills training and self-regulation

    03

    SOCIAL SUPPORT

    Social support is the thrust from the surrounding communities; It can serve as the source of information and emotional support; the successful predecessor can also become the social model

    SOLUTIONS

    After synthesizing our findins from the two-step research, we came up with four solutions.

    Create a "game" featured with achieving and rewarding mechanismWhen users achieve a setting goal(e.g., finishing a tutorial session), they will be rewarded some virtual material for building their virtue home in the game, making the practice more engaging and so they want to keep the practice.

    Focus on teaching techniques rather than emphasizing special interestsUsers switch in different tutorials serving for different interests, but eventually feel disappointed to find they are all talking about the same thing. We want to reduce that confusion and focus on teaching users how to meditate.

    Enable heart beat rate tracking with wearable devices We want to make users feel that the efforts they have put in do transfer into positive impacts to their life. To achieve this goal, we will present data with clean and easy-to-understand visual design, so users can see the change meditation bring to their body and being more motivated to persist with the practice

    Incorporate a robust social community platform When users find the current practice challenging, there should be a place for them to seek advices and find their belongings. In this section, there are going to be clipboards allowing users to share information, an in-app message function, and an event function.
