COURSE CATALOG

A project for redesigning UT course catalog website

OVERVIEW

This is an ongoing project. The aim of the project is to enhance the students' experiences for course searching and class registration. I will keep updating in this semster. You are welcomed to come back to check the progress.

DESIGN PROCESS

BACKGORUND

Although its importance for every UT student, unfortuantely the UT course catalog has long been complained for its outdated and lack of integration. The course search criteria used by the current website do not meet the need for more precise filtering. In addition, there is no further action student can take on the search result page, such as adding a course to planner or bookmarking. Students still have to take notes of the course numbers by hand and put those numbers on the registration web page, which is seperated from the course search website.

Being the student of UT, we know those painpoints well. It intrigures us to initiate a redesign project of the UT course catalog. On the other hand, from the perspective of Information Architecture, we are also interested in what and how students usually look for when composing their class schedule. We are going to take a both content and user centered approach to tackle those research questions.

SUMMARY

Our project focuses on redesigning the UT course registration process, combining both the registration workflow as well as course catalog selection. We utilized several different research methods to reach our final set of design recommendations. Some of the methods we employed include: competitive analysis, content inventory, persona development, interviews, card sorting, and finally, affinity diagram. We then designed and tested mid-fidelity wireframes to further hone our final deliverables. We used Adobe XD to design prototypes and ran usability tests on our design. With emphasis on information architecture, this project provided us with a deepened understanding of the importance of information architecture in the overall design of an intuitive and effective user interface.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

We started our research through conducting competitive analysis at 15 institutions, including UT. The initial competitive analysis showed us numerous approaches universities took to create their course catalog system. While we would have liked to interact more closely with the registration process at various universities, course registration at all the universities we analyzed required login information. Because of this, bulk of the analysis focused on the course catalog. Though we were able to observe general registration procedures through tutorial videos universities provided.

The four major parts we analyzed are:

01

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Organizational system, navigational system, organizational structure, and metadata.

02

SEARCH FUNCTIONALITY

How the site designed the course search experience

03

VISUAL DESIGN

The overall refinement of visual design for the site.

04

ADDITIONAL FEATURES

Any outstanding features besides the bare minimum course search process.
