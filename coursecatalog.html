COURSE CATALOG

A project for redesigning UT course catalog website

OVERVIEW

This is an ongoing project. The aim of the project is to enhance the students' experiences for course searching and class registration. I will keep updating in this semster. You are welcomed to come back to check the progress.

DESIGN PROCESS

BACKGORUND

Although it has importance to every UT student, unfortuantely, the UT course catalog has long been complained for its outdated and lack of integration. The course search criteria used by the current website do not meet the need for more precise filtering. In addition, there is no further action student can take on the search result page, such as adding a course to planner or bookmarking. Students still have to take notes of the course numbers by hand and put those numbers on the registration web page, which is seperated from the course search website.

Being the student of UT, we know those painpoints well. It intrigures us to initiate a redesign project of the UT course catalog. On the other hand, from the perspective of Information Architecture, we are also interested in what and how students usually look for when composing their class schedule. We are going to take a both content and user centered approach to tackle those research questions.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

In order to learn more about how course catalog and register systems are like, and how they work, we firstly conducted a competitive analysis. We selected 15 insititutions in and out of the United States, including UT. Through the competitive analysis, we found there are numerous approaches those insititutions adopted to create course catalog systems. On register system, however, we had trouble to obtain access due to the exclusion of visistors.Because of this, the competitive analysis mainly focuses on course catalog. Though we were able to observe general registration procedures through tutorial videos universities provided.

The four major parts we analyzed are:

01

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

Organizational system, navigational system, organizational structure, and metadata.

02

SEARCH FUNCTIONALITY

How the site designed the course search experience

03

VISUAL DESIGN

The overall refinement of visual design for the site.

04

ADDITIONAL FEATURES

Any outstanding features besides the bare minimum course search process.
